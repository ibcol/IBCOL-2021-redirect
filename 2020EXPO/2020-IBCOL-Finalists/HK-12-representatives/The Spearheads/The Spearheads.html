Project Description

Our team proposes a project idea named “Spearchain”, which is a track-and-trace platform that aims to leverage blockchain together with other technologies to oversee the delivery process of medication donations. By taking on the features of blockchain that it is decentralized, verifiable, immutable, and traceable, we are confident that our project could help diminish the corruption problem in the charity sector.

A host of medical equipment manufacturers, healthcare products distributors, and the pharmaceutical companies donate medical materials to hospitals via non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and charities regularly. However, there is a severe corruption problem in the philanthropy sector. There are many cases seen that civil society organizations are unable to obtain offerings. An example can be seen from several years ago when the Haiti Earthquake happened, fundraising and supplies collections actions were immediately taken. Nevertheless, a substantial portion of the US relief operation went through American instead of Haiti-owned companies.

In order to enhance the transparency of the medical donation delivery process, we intend to partner with donors, NGOs, recipients, regulators, and technological companies. The mechanism of Spearchain is simple that when donors plan to donate pharmaceutical equipment, we will provide our “smart boxes”, which have internally-installed sensors to store and encrypt their supplies. The sensors can detect the locations and storage conditions of items, as well as upload the information onto our cloud system. If the sensors spot any abnormal situations, they will transmit and save the data onto our blockchain platform. In this way, all the related system users can view and access the data. Receiving health supplies, recipients need to decrypt the boxes by using private keys. Following that, the transactions will complete, the smart contracts will be terminated and recorded on the blockchain system. We acquire revenue by charging donors annual platform subscription fees.

It is anticipated that our project can help identify corruption cases and underhand dealings easily and quickly. Because the whole delivery process is transparent, visible, and decentralized, our system will hopefully ensure that the in-kind offers are donated to intended recipients, escalating donors' confidence to donate supplies. Meanwhile, we aim to promote responsibility among NGOs since they now know that they cannot get away with unethical dealings. It is believed that our project is not only feasible but also meaningful.

This whitepaper covers the vision and opportunity, market analysis, partners, competitors, potential risks and solutions, technology and architecture, governance, value proposition, distribution, and conclusion.
